Gastronomy Highlight
Northern Origin

Phở

Vietnam's iconic rice noodle soup featuring aromatic broth, fresh herbs, and tender meat (usually beef or chicken).

Nationwide

Spring Rolls

Both fresh (gỏi cuốn) and fried (chả giò) varieties filled with vegetables, herbs, and protein, served with dipping sauce.

Rice paper Shrimp/Pork Fresh herbs Dipping sauce
Northern Specialty

Bún Chả

Hanoi specialty featuring grilled pork served with rice noodles, herbs, and a flavorful dipping sauce. Made famous by Anthony Bourdain and Barack Obama.

Grilled pork Rice noodles Fish sauce Fresh herbs
Hoi An Specialty

Cao Lầu

Unique to Hoi An with distinctive noodles, sliced pork, crispy croutons, bean sprouts, and fresh herbs. Traditionally made with water from the ancient Ba Le well.

Thick rice noodles Char siu pork Crispy croutons Local herbs

Regional Variations

Northern Cuisine:

Subtle flavors, less spicy, more balanced. Emphasis on freshness with light seasoning.

Central Cuisine:

Spicier dishes with complex preparations. Imperial influence from Hue with elaborate presentation.

Southern Cuisine:

Sweeter dishes with more herbs, tropical fruits, and influences from Cambodia and Thailand.

Did You Know?

Vietnamese cuisine follows the five fundamental taste elements: spicy, sour, bitter, salty, and sweet

Fish sauce (nước mắm) is the key ingredient that gives many dishes their distinctive umami flavor

Fresh herbs are essential in Vietnamese cooking, with most dishes garnished with a variety of aromatic herbs
