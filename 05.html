Discover Vietnam's rich heritage through its most significant historical sites

Cultural Heritage
UNESCO World Heritage

Imperial City of Hue

Former imperial capital of the Nguyen Dynasty (1802-1945), featuring a walled citadel, palaces, temples, and royal tombs along the Perfume River.

Established: Early 19th century
Location: Central Vietnam
Highlight: The Purple Forbidden City
UNESCO World Heritage

My Son Sanctuary

Ancient Hindu temple complex built by the Champa Kingdom between the 4th and 13th centuries, showing Indian cultural influences in architecture.

Built: 4th to 13th century CE
Location: Near Hoi An, Central Vietnam
Highlight: Brick towers with Sanskrit inscriptions

Cu Chi Tunnels

Vast network of underground tunnels used by Viet Cong soldiers during the Vietnam War, featuring living quarters, hospitals, and command centers.

Built: 1940s-1960s
Location: Northwest of Ho Chi Minh City
Highlight: 250km network of interconnected tunnels

Hoa Lu Ancient Capital

Vietnam's first feudal capital during the Dinh and Le dynasties (10th century), nestled among limestone mountains with ancient temples and fortifications.

Period: 968-1009 CE
Location: Ninh Binh Province
Highlight: King Dinh and King Le temples

Historical Significance:

Vietnam's historical sites reflect the country's complex past, from ancient Hindu-influenced kingdoms to imperial dynasties and modern conflicts. These sites provide insights into Vietnam's resilience, cultural adaptations, and architectural achievements throughout its 4,000-year history.
